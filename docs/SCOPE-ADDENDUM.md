# ADDENDUM — "THE ACADEMY" (philosophy)
## Engagement, addiction-to-growth, and philosophy that follows you into your actual life

> **Addendum to the Academy scope.** Philosophy's version of "changing how you watch movies" is bigger: **changing how you think — and noticing it happen.** The Commitment Map already gives this app the best long-term hook in the suite (a worldview that accretes for years); this addendum builds the daily loop, the visible ladder, and — the distinctive piece — the bridge between the seminar and the user's *actual life*: their arguments, their news feed, their decisions. Tiers: **[E1]**, **[E2]**, **[E3]**.

---

## 1. The Life — philosophy applied to *your* life (the distinctive system)

### 1.1 "Bring me an argument" ⭐ **[E1]**
The killer real-world feature. The user brings a live argument from their life — a disagreement with a friend, a take they read, a decision they're wrestling with — and the professor does what philosophers actually do: **extracts the structure.** What's the actual claim? What premise is doing the work? Where do you and your friend *really* diverge (facts? values? definitions?)? The user leaves with the argument mapped — often discovering the disagreement was about something else entirely. This is the "explain what I'm hearing" of philosophy: it proves the app's value on material the user already cares about, and it *trains transfer* — the whole point. Guardrails: the professor dissects arguments, never referees relationships or gives life advice; it maps reasoning, and hands judgment back.

### 1.2 The News, Read Philosophically **[E2]**
A weekly session: one live public question (an AI-rights story, a free-speech case, a medical-ethics dilemma from the news) run through the frameworks the user has learned — "you've studied Mill; what would harm-principle reasoning say here? Now Kant. Now notice where they split." Uses web search for the story; the philosophy stays grounded in the ingested canon. Positions taken feed the Commitment Map. Presented even-handedly by construction — the *method* is showing multiple frameworks, never delivering a verdict.

### 1.3 The Practice Wing (lived philosophy) **[E2]**
Prof. Bede's Stoic course becomes a *practice*: a morning intention, an evening examen (2 minutes: "what disturbed you today, and was it in your control?"), negative-visualization exercises, journaled and lightly reviewed in seminar. Philosophy-as-a-way-of-life is the oldest engagement loop in the discipline (it retained Marcus Aurelius); framed as practice, never as therapy.

## 2. The Loop — the daily ritual **[E1]**
- **The Daily Question:** one question each morning, small enough to think about while the coffee brews — "Is a copy of you *you*?" "Can a promise bind your future self?" One-tap position + one sentence why. The professor responds once. Positions accrete to the Commitment Map — so the daily ritual literally *builds your worldview object*, sixty seconds at a time. This is the Wordle of the suite's daily rituals with the deepest payoff.
- **Thought-experiment drops:** the branching intuition-pump labs released weekly as shareable events ("this week: the Experience Machine"). Compare your choice against the (anonymous, aggregate) crowd after — "68% plugged in; you didn't; here's the divide."
- Rolling-ratio streaks; the notification always *contains* the question (never "come back!").

## 3. The Ladder — visible growth **[E2]**
- **The Worldview page as the progress object:** territory mapped (domains you've examined vs. untouched — "you have no epistemology yet; everything you believe rests on a theory of knowledge you haven't met"), tensions open and *resolved* (resolving one is a celebrated event), and **the changelog of your mind** — the positions you've abandoned, with dates and the argument that moved you. "You've changed your mind 4 times this year, each under pressure of argument" is the proudest stat in the suite.
- **The re-encounter:** the same thought experiment re-run a year later, your two answers side by side. Growth made visible as self-difference.
- **Steelman scores:** can you state the opposing view so well its holders would sign it? Graded exercise, ranked levels. The rarest skill on the internet, made a ladder.

## 4. Social-lite **[E3]**
- **The Symposium:** a monthly live-ish debate event — one question, the community takes positions, the faculty argue the sides, aggregate movement shown ("this argument moved 22% of you").
- **Dinner-party packs:** exportable question cards for actual human conversation — the app deliberately pushing philosophy *off* the screen.

## 5. Engagement ethics (heightened here)
A worldview app must never become an ideology app: the faculty's structural disagreement is the safeguard, the map records *your* positions without ever scoring them right/wrong, and abandonment of positions is framed as the *point* of the practice. No belief-nudging, no "most users think..." framing on live moral questions (aggregates shown only after you've answered, as description not pressure). Sessions conclude; aporia ends with reflection, not a cliffhanger designed to reopen the app.

## 6. Build order
1. **E-M1:** Daily Question → Commitment Map wiring + "Bring me an argument."
2. **E-M2:** Worldview-as-progress (changelog, tensions, territory) + thought-experiment weekly drops + steelman ladder.
3. **E-M3:** News-read-philosophically + the Practice Wing + re-encounters.
4. **E-M4:** Symposium events + dinner-party packs.
